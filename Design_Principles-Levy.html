Design Pinciples

Key Principles

  • Make visibility a priority.
  • Be consistent.
  • Give immediate feedback.
  • Use constraints to help users.
  • Use affordances and signifiers.
  • Make your product accessible.

Complexity

  • Tesler's Law - Cannot reduce all complexity.
  • Make important information visually salient.
  • Provide fluid pathways.
  • Use things like to tool tips to increase efficiency.
  • Reduce clutter.
  • Caused by dependencies, obscurities and redundancy.

Cultural Considerations

  • Use colors and signs carefully, they mean different things to different cultures.
  • Give your website many languages so that many different cultures can use it.
  • Don't assume that the cultures are the same because they are close to each other.
  • Know if the culture responds well to authority.
  • Do user research to find out more about the culture!

Conceptual

  • Design the model purposefully, don't let the technology determine the model.
  • Make sure the designer's model, the user model, and the system image match!
  • Be careful of the legacy issue, make sure to not keep going back to old designs if they are bad.
  • Use metaphors to make the model simple.
  • If you can't easily explain the model, it's bad!

Cognition

  • Memory: Limit how much people can remember. Most people can only remember 7 things at a time!
  • Attention: Do NOT force people to focus for long times! People pay attention to salient clues.
  • Perception: Use familiar designs, people scan things based on past experience.
  • Learning: Make sure your product allows users to make mistakes when learning.

Accessibility

  • Make sure that anyone can perceive your website, through visuals and voice assistance.
  • Use more than color to get ideas across, otherwise colorblind people will hate your website!
  • Use appropriate texts and fonts to make it so that your website is easy to read and use.
  • Design for multiple interfaces so that more people can access your product.

Emotional

  • Make using your product an enjoyable experience.
  • Have surprises to delight your users instead of boring them.
  • Have empathy for your users!
  • Do NOT be unethical and manipulate your users into making choices when they are vulnerable.

Error Principles

  • Do everything possible to prevent errors.
  • Give detailed error messages that explain what went wrong, and how to fix it.
  • Find the root causes of your errors (5 whys).
  • Know the differences between slips (action/memory based) and mistakes (rule/knowledge based).

Feedback

  • Make feedback immediate!
  • Don't use too much feedback, you will confuse your users.
  • Use haptic feedback sparingly.
  • Make feedback clear and concise.

Discoverability/Findability

  • Make it easy for a user to find everything.
  • Use surveys, heat maps, and analytics to see what users are focused on.
  • Use progressive disclosure! (Show a bit at a time)
  • Have contextual onboarding. (Adapt to users)
